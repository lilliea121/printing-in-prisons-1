The man who confessed that he evaded military registration because be was afraid to fight, ought to be exempted on the ground of mental and moral deficiency. He is not of the stuff of which good soldiers are made.

But if he were to be decorated with a fitting badge of exemption for cowardice, a few weeks of privileged freedom might serve to change his mind.
