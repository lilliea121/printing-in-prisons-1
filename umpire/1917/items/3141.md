Machinery has been shipped from the State of Washington to Honolulu by four men who are making an investigation of the use of the fibre from the banana trunk for bag making. Heretofore the sugar planters of the Hawaiian Islands used rags brought from Calcutta as containers for the raw sugar product, but war conditions and uncertainty in the matter of shipments have compelled them to look about for a substitute.
