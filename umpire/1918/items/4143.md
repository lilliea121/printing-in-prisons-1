“Failure is in a sense the high-way to success, in as much as every discovery of what is false leads us to seek earnestly after what is true, and every fresh experience points out some form of error which we shall afterward carefully avoid.” — Keats

Aug.19 1918.

The regular meeting of the Board of Governors was called to order by it’s Chairman B 8436, on the 19th of August, 1918 at1 P. M.

The minutes of the preceding meeting, August 5th, 1918, were read, and approved.

There were five members present.

The secretary then read the report of the Representative Delegates. Upon motion made by B 8762, and seconded by 9017, the report was accepted. Application for memberships were received and approved as follows: B 8297, 8066, 9301, 9305, 9306, 9308, 9309

The following communications were received during the week. From B 8337 requesting the loan of $5.00 on his release. The loan was refused, but a railroad ticket to his home was granted him. From B 8583, requesting the loan of $3.00 was refused.

From B 8970 requesting the loan of $6.50 to aid his family was granted and on motion the Vice President and Secretary was appointed a committee of two to take up this man’s case with the Warden.

Receipts for the week ending August 17, 1918 were as follows.

W.S.S 1.00
To B 8156 0.25
To Rev. Joseph Welch for paper. 0.54
Loan at the Gate to B 8766. 2.00

No further business being present, the motion to adjourn was moved by B 9017.

Carried. 

B 9017 Secretary.
