In its editorial columns the New Era comments that it is just as easy to get out of this place with sentimental letter-writing as it is to convince the officials you deserve a 21-gun salute fired in your honor on the day of your discharge. Yes, yes! But how aree we going to impress that fact on the host of writers who fail to see that a little applied personal effort is worth loads of glittering generalities?
