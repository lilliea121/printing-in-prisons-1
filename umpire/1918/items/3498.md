Philadelphia, Pa. 
January 12, 1918.

B 8266, Secretary,
Honor & Friendship Club, 
Eastern State Penitentiary, Phila.,
My dear Secretary:

Acknowledging your favor advising me that I have been elected to Honorary Membership in the Honor and Friendship Club I beg to state I consider it a privilege to be invited to accept membership in your Club.

I am in thorough sympathy with the purpose of your Club and desire to do all in my power to ascist every effort put forth for the betterment of mankind. I believe you and your colleagues are doing splendid work and it is my pleasure to give my personal endorsement to your work, believing that the influence for good surrounding, the men of your Club, by reason of the Principles advocated by it, will result in a lasting benefit not only to your members, but will extend beyond its confines and in a measure will be a lasting benefit to mankind.

Kindly convey my good wishes to your Officers and members and state that I look forward with pleasure to meeting you some time to express in person my pleasure at being elected an Honorary member of your Club.

With kindest thoughts and best wishes believe me, 

Very truly yours, 

E. J. LAFFERTY.
