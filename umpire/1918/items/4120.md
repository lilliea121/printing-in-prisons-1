WAR WORK FOR PRISONERS

Massachusetts May Parole Selected Men to Work in Munition Plants.

Boston, Aug. 3.-Prisoners in Massachusetts penal institutions may be paroled to go into munition plants and do other kinds of war work. If they make good they will be given full pardons, with the privilege of enlisting for active military service.

Appeals have reached the Governor from prisoners who said they had subscribed liberally to Liberty Loan bonds, to the Red Cross and other patriotic campaigns and begged for a chance furiber to demonstrate their love of country by enlisting in the army.

Governor Mc Call and Frank A Brooks. of Williamsburg, chairman of the State Board of Parole, are giving careful consideration to a plan to try out selected prisoners, Chairman Brooks has been delegated ly the Governor to investigate conditions in States where this plan is being worked out.
