William Gibbs McAdoo, Secretary of the Treasury, Director-General: of Railroads, holder of a number of other jobs, including that of being son-in-law to President Wilson, suddenly resigns, giving as his reason, the inadequacy of his salary, and the necessity of providing for his family by securing a more lucrative form of employment, and the political pot thereby boils furiously.
