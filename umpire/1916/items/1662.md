JUDGE CLARK NAMED FOR U. S. SUPREME COURT

The expected nomination of Judge John Hessin Clarke, of Cleveland, O., to be an asociate Justice of the Supreme Court of the United State to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of Charles E. Hughes, was sent to the Senate by President Wilson Saturday. The nominee, who isa staunch Democrat, is the Federal Judge for the Northern Judicial District of Ohio. He was a Democrat candidate for the United States Senate against Marcus A. Hanna.

Judge Clarke is a blachelor. He will be 59 years old in September. The only bachelor on the Supreme bench is associate Justice James C McReynolds.

Judge Clark's nomination was urged by Secretary of War Baker, with whom he was associate in politics in Cleveleand.

The new Justice was born an Lisbon, Ohio, graduated at Western Reserve University in 1875, and was admitted to the Ohio bar in 1878. Up to the time of his appointment as District Judge in 1914, he practiced law in the courts of Ohio.
