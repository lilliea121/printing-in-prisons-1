IRONY OF FATE    Thousands of peasants and princes have in the reign of the mighty Czar of Russia been sent to the awful mines and the wilds of Siberia, and there toiled away their existence in misery and sadness. What a balm to their wronged and aching hearts must it not have been to see this mighty Czar delivered over to them in chains, dethroned and humiliated, giving them their liberty, and the gratification to hear the shot that ended for all time the autocracy in their homeland.
